The Vision
So hopefully you are reading this after having had a look at the rest of the timeline though I wouldn't blame you if you skipped it, it takes a while to read. I wanted to, at the end of all of this, reassert what I am trying to do here. The end goal is to have students that need to take the SAT or the ACT (or any other standardized test for that matter) look on Amazon or in Best Buy and right next to the $150 graphing calculators, see a $50 dollar option with as many necessary features and good reviews as all of the big brands. It's a tough task, but even as I head into the next stage of my life with university, it's a challenge I am going to continue to pursue, all because a calculator cost too much.

You thought that was a conclusion huh? No, I have a bit more to say.
Moving Forward
There are a few things that I want to mention regarding the future, some are core aspects of the project, some are just in regards to the physical design of the calculator.
The first thing I want to talk about is the next stage of prototyping.

The single biggest drawback to my previous prototype is my compute unit. Using a Raspberry Pi is fundamentally against the vision of this project as it is an incredibly open piece of hardware. Not only does it hold a full OS that bad actors could get into, the whole point of something like a Rasperry Pi is that you have control over you hardware, something that would also be a bad idea to give a random student control over.
